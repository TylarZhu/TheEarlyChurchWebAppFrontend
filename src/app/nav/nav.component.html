Brief History

Christianity came out of Judaism. In the beginning, Christianity was not yet called Christianity, nor were Christians called Christians. The place where they met and worshiped God was still in the synagogue. Still, they chose an Old Testament word to call themselves - "the church" (a group of people called by God).

Their greatest struggle at this point was: Did they still belong to Judaism, or had they been set apart? If they were set apart, what would we be in the future?

They were mixed up with other Jews. On the one hand, the Jewish authorities were aware of this group's differences, so they tried hard to suppress them. Once they found out, they expelled them from the synagogue (at that time, if Jews were expelled from the synagogue, it was equivalent to 'social death'); on the other hand, this group had to work hard to clean out those mixed people, and to form their own "church."
